Remote Desktop, SSH, SFTP, and private web — one iPad app.

TermiForge is an iPad app for remotely using your Mac and your servers. It replaces four separate tools: a Remote Desktop client for controlling your Mac's screen, an SSH Terminal for running commands, an SFTP File Manager for editing remote files, and a private Web browser for pages that only your Mac or your network can see.

What's inside

Four tools. One app. One connection per host.

Stop switching between a Remote Desktop app, an SSH app, an SFTP app, and a browser. TermiForge does all four, sharing a single authenticated SSH session per host.

Flagship

Remote Desktop

View and control your Mac's screen from your iPad. Full trackpad, hardware-keyboard, Apple Pencil, and MFi game-controller input. Wakes your Mac automatically if it's sleeping or at the login screen.

SSH Terminal

A real xterm-256color shell for running commands on any SSH server. Tab completion, ANSI colors, scrollback, full hardware-keyboard shortcuts. Sessions stay alive when you switch apps.

SFTP File Manager

Browse, upload, rename, and edit files on any SSH server. Tap "Open With…" on any file to hand it to Textastic, Runestone, or any iPad editor — your edits flow back to the server automatically.

Private Web

A browser for pages only your Mac or your network can see — localhost:3000, a Jupyter notebook, your router admin page, an internal company dashboard. Tunneled over SSH. No exposed ports, no VPN.

Why Remote Desktop is different

Built to feel like a connected display,
not a streaming video.

Remote Desktop is the flagship feature. These are the specific things we do that make it fast and pleasant to use, where most remote-desktop apps cut corners.

Under one frame of added latency on LAN

H.264 encoded on the Mac's GPU, decoded on the iPad's GPU — no CPU re-encoding in between. Your Mac's fans don't spin up.

Adaptive bitrate

A brief network hiccup drops the quality for a second and recovers. No black screens, no frozen frames, no manual reconnect.

Wakes a sleeping Mac

Mac display off, or screen locked? The connection wakes it on-demand so your session starts right away. No extra apps, no VNC detour.

Input that actually works

Apple Pencil pressure, two-finger trackpad scroll, hardware Cmd-Tab, and MFi controller buttons all reach your Mac the way they should.

On the iPad side

Edit remote files
like they're local.

Tap "Open With…" on any file in the SFTP browser and hand it straight to your favorite iPad editor. Save in the editor — the file uploads back to the server the moment it's written. No intermediate folder, no dragging around, no forgetting to sync.

  • Works with Textastic, Runestone, and anything else that supports open-in-place.
  • Hidden dotfiles (.ssh/config, .bashrc) are fully editable.
  • Edits to binary files (images, PDFs) round-trip as cleanly as text.

macOS Agent

Install on your Mac in under a minute.

A tiny menu-bar app that lets your iPad connect for Remote Desktop. Does nothing until you connect — sits idle, uses no CPU. One drag-and-drop install, signed, and local-network by default.

  1. Download the DMG from Releases.
  2. Open it and drag TermiForgeAgent to Applications.
  3. Launch it. Grant Screen Recording and Accessibility when prompted.
  4. Connect from your iPad — done.

Privacy isn't a page we wrote.
It's how the product is built.

No accounts. No cloud.

TermiForge connects your iPad directly to your servers. There is no middle tier — we don't operate one.

Credentials stay on-device.

SSH keys, passwords, and your Mac login live in iOS Keychain with device-only accessibility. Never synced to iCloud, never uploaded.

End-to-end encrypted.

SSH for Terminal, SFTP, and Private Web. DTLS with a fresh per-session key for Remote Desktop. Your frames, keystrokes, and files never pass through our infrastructure.
